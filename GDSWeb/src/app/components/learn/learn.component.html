Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS-15)

What is the GDS-15?

The Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS) is a valuable tool used to assess depression, particularly in older adults. Unlike other scales, the GDS minimizes the influence of physical illness on results by focusing on the emotional and cognitive aspects of depression, rather than somatic symptoms, which may overlap with physical health conditions.

The questions in the GDS are simple and designed to be easily understood by individuals with cognitive impairments, ensuring that responses are straightforward and meaningful.

What does my score mean?
  • 0-5: Normal
  • 5-15: Risk of Depression
    • When do we use this test?
    This tool is commonly used in clinical settings to screen for depression in elderly patients, track symptom progression over time, and monitor responses to treatment.
